## Define Your Requirements First

Before you start evaluating agencies, get clarity on what you actually need. Document your project scope, timeline, and budget range. Identify which skills are essential &mdash; do you need mobile expertise, AI capabilities, or deep domain knowledge in your industry? The more specific you are about your requirements, the easier it becomes to distinguish between agencies that can genuinely deliver and those that simply claim they can.

## Evaluating Technical Competence

Look beyond the sales pitch. Ask to see case studies with measurable results, not just screenshots. Request references from clients with similar project scopes. Review their code quality if possible &mdash; open-source contributions, technical blog posts, and architecture documentation all reveal more about an agency&apos;s engineering culture than a polished portfolio page. Pay attention to how they discuss trade-offs and limitations. Any agency that promises everything without caveats is selling you something other than software development.

## Communication and Process

The best technical team in the world will fail if communication breaks down. During your evaluation, pay close attention to how quickly and clearly the agency responds. Ask about their development process: do they use agile sprints, how do they handle scope changes, and what does their reporting look like? Insist on regular demos of working software &mdash; not just status updates. The agencies that deliver consistently are the ones that make collaboration easy and keep you informed at every step.

## Pricing Models and Red Flags

Understand the pricing model before you commit. Fixed-price contracts work for well-defined projects with stable requirements. Time-and-materials contracts are better for complex projects where requirements will evolve. Be wary of agencies that quote dramatically below market rates &mdash; the savings often come at the cost of junior developers, poor code quality, and expensive maintenance down the road.
