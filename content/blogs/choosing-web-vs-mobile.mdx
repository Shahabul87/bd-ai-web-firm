## The Decision Framework

Choosing between a web app and a mobile app is one of the most important technical decisions a business can make. The wrong choice can mean wasted budget, poor user engagement, and months of rework. The right choice depends on your audience, your feature requirements, and your timeline.

## When to Build a Web App

Web apps shine when you need broad reach without friction. Users access them through a browser with no download required, which dramatically lowers the barrier to entry. If your product is content-heavy, relies on SEO for discovery, or needs to work across every device without platform-specific development, a web app is typically the stronger choice. Progressive Web Apps (PWAs) have closed much of the gap with native apps, offering offline support, push notifications, and home-screen installation.

## When to Build a Mobile App

Native mobile apps are the right call when your product depends on deep device integration &mdash; camera access, GPS, biometrics, background processing, or heavy graphics. If your users will interact with the product daily and expect a polished, fast experience, native apps deliver performance that web apps struggle to match. App store distribution also provides a built-in discovery channel and a level of trust that matters in industries like finance and healthcare.

## A Practical Approach

For most startups and small businesses, we recommend starting with a web app. It is faster and cheaper to build, easier to iterate on, and reaches the widest audience. Once you validate your product-market fit and identify specific mobile-only features your users need, you can invest in a native app with confidence.
